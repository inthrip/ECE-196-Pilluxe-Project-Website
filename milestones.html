Project Milestones and Timeline

This page lays out the testable hypothesis, timeline figure, and concrete deliverables for the project. Each milestone should describe what will be completed, not just what work will begin.

Testable Hypothesis

When users utilize this device to assist in taking medication, reminders remove the constant memorization and the incentivization system makes the act of taking feel rewarding rather than tedious; this ultimately improves medication adherence rates among users.

Timeline Figure

Timeline of project milestones and completion dates.

Milestones

These milestones are written as specific deliverables. They describe what will be functioning or completed by each checkpoint.

  1. PCB design completed with Wi-Fi and Bluetooth capabilities integrated.
  2. Mobile app implemented and successfully communicating with the PCB.
  3. CAD model finished and the pill container 3D printed with openings for LEDs and push buttons.
  4. App configured with visual design, day-by-day streaks, and an incentive-based reward system.
  5. Optional: report or diary system implemented so users can track their condition and medication progress over time.
  6. Testing phase completed, with the pillbox and app used as intended to identify flaws and evaluate whether the system reduces tedium and feels enjoyable to use.

What the instructor wants

  • A figure of the timeline
  • Specific dates for when something specific will be completed
  • Milestones written as outcomes, not vague work phases

Writing Guidance

Do not write milestones like “begin software development.” Write milestones like “app successfully sends a notification when the scheduled medication time is reached.”
