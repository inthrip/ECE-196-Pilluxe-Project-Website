Milestone schedule

What completion looks like.

  1. Wk 5 · Fri
    Milestone 01 Completed on Time

    PCB Fully Designed

    Our PCB was fully designed and ordered, implemeneting all the intended features: Bluetooth capability, through-holees for connecting to external LEDs and buttons, an IMU module, and rechargeability for the battery. No significant issues or pivots.

  2. Wk 6 · Fri
    Milestone 02 Completed on Time

    BLE Communication Completed

    iOS to ESP32 BLE communication was fully implemented (LightBlue/Arduino Sketch) and our Phone was able to successfully read button presses from our DevBoard and light LEDs connected to our DevBoard over BLE. Implementing this was actually slightly easier than expected.

  3. Wk 7 · Fri
    Milestone 03 Completed with Small Delay

    Bare iOS App Completed

    We were able to implement and run a very basic app on our iPhone. Our previously designed BLE communication was scaffolded to be compatible with our app, allowing our app to be able to communicate over BLE to control our DevBoard, limited to only lighting and adjusting LEDs and registering buttons presses. This milestone experciened slight delays due to issues debugging Swift code and setting up Xcode.

  4. Wk 9 · Mon
    Milestone 04 Completed with Small Delay

    App Incetivization System and User-Customization Implemented (App Completed)

    Our app's final intended features were successfully implemented: dose and daily streaks act as incentivization, users are able to customize their box LEDs as well as the app visuals, All app controls were verified on our soldered PCB.

Challenges we've faced

When the plan met reality.

Not everything has gone smoothly. Partway through the project, one of the components for our PCB was misordered — a small clerical mistake with an outsized timeline consequence. The correct part took several extra days to arrive, which postponed the soldering of our PCB and pushed back the chain of dependent tasks behind it.

The setback was its own lesson. We've learned to be meticulous with every aspect of the project — verifying part specifications, double-checking orders, confirming numbers — long before any soldering iron comes out.
